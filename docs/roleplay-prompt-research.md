# DECA role-play prompt research

Research date: July 20, 2026
Competition year reviewed: 2026–2027

This document records the evidence used by DECA Pal's event-specific practice generator. It is a product-research artifact, not an official DECA publication. Generated cases must be labeled as unofficial practice material and must never reproduce an official case.

## What the official materials establish

The [official competitive-events overview](https://www.deca.org/compete) describes four role-play families in this product: Principles, Individual Series, Personal Financial Literacy and Team Decision Making. The current official guidelines and evaluation forms produce these practice rules:

| Family | Participants | Preliminaries / finals | Prep / presentation | PIs | Current 100-point practice rubric |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---|
| Individual Series | 1 | 2 role-plays / third for finalists | 10 / 10 min | 5 | PIs 50, Solution 24, Career Competencies 18, Overall 8 |
| Principles | 1 | 1 role-play / second for finalists | 10 / 10 min | 4 | PIs 48, Solution 24, Career Competencies 18, Overall 10 |
| Team Decision Making | 2 | 1 case / second for finalists | 30 / 15 min | 5 | PIs 50, Solution 24, Career Competencies 18, Overall 8 |
| Personal Financial Literacy | 1 | 1 role-play / second for finalists | 10 / 10 min | 3 | PIs 51, Solution 24, Career Competencies 18, Overall 7 |

### Team Decision Making season change

Team Decision Making case studies used **seven performance indicators in the 2025–2026 format**. The 2026–2027 guidelines and sample evaluation form now use **five performance indicators plus three separately scored Solution criteria** (Unique, Practical and Effective). This application targets 2026–2027, so its live generator uses five PIs. Historical seven-PI samples remain useful for topic-frequency research, but their rubric shape must not be copied into current simulations.

## How the research becomes event-specific prompts

`app/data/roleplayProfiles.ts` contains one profile for every one of the 28 available events. Each profile defines:

- the official event family and paraphrased event scope;
- its descriptive public-case frequency mix;
- four to six event-specific scenario archetypes;
- plausible participant roles, judge roles, meeting settings, required tasks and complications;
- event-specific guardrails, such as internally consistent calculations for ACT/BFS, two-sided analysis without invented legal conclusions for BLTDM, and guest-safety realism for hospitality events.

Random instructional-area selection uses the observed case-bank counts as variety weights when the labels match the local PI dataset. Archetype weights provide variation inside the event. A user can still select a specific instructional area, which overrides that random mix.

The final system prompt is assembled by `app/utils/roleplayPromptBuilder.ts`. The server—not the model—selects the event, format, exact PI count, exact PIs, Career Competencies, timing and 100-point rubric. The model only writes the original situation and mirrored judge instructions. Every event prompt requires a fictional organization, concrete facts and constraints, an implementation decision, exactly two judge questions, no outside research, no copied official material and an explicit unofficial-practice label.
